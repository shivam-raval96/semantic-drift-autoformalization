# 09 — Synthetic laws beyond the ETP cap: complexity to 10 ops/equation

Run 2026-07-22.

## Question

Every experiment so far samples the ETP's 4,694 laws, which stop at 4
operations per equation, so the accuracy-vs-complexity curves of
experiments 02–07 end where the list does. Does the decline measured on
ETP pairs continue, cliff, or floor when equation complexity is
extended to 10 operations per equation — and do experiment 07's no-think
findings (literal dominates; two-stage's flat curve overtakes the
single-shot arms as complexity grows; the story arm is where frontier
models still lose pairs) persist past the cap? A frontier pair —
GPT-5.5 and Opus 4.8 — joins the eight standard models to see whether
the near-saturation GPT-5.5 showed on ETP-range pairs survives 2.5×
the complexity.

The ETP list is the *complete* enumeration of laws up to 4 operations,
so every synthetic law in bins m ≤ 4 necessarily coincides with a
public ETP law (up to renaming and side swap) — but every law in bins
m ≥ 5 is outside any published list. The m = 4→5 boundary is therefore
a memorization check: if models were leaning on the public ETP list,
accuracy should cliff there rather than decline smoothly.

## Setup

Same pipeline, prompts, grading, and regime machinery as experiment 07;
the changed variables are the equation source (synthetic), the
stratification (balanced per-equation bins), and the added Opus 4.8.

- **Equations**: `data/synthetic-equations.txt` (committed; sha256
  `7f11f36c9d0df69361d47f934887f9bec42c82f4c4271de1b8344e4da1ad9ce0`),
  364 laws generated by `genform.py --seed 9 --bins 1:10 --per-bin 40`:
  for each per-equation operation count m = 1..10, up to 40 distinct
  laws (bin 1 saturates at its full 5 — exactly ETP E3–E7 — and bin 2
  at its full 39). Uniform tree shapes per side (Catalan-weighted
  splits), ETP-style first-appearance variables capped at the
  renderers' 6 letters, sides normalized smaller-first, laws deduped up
  to renaming + side swap (duals kept distinct), no vacuous or
  identical-sided laws by construction.
- **Models** (10, via OpenRouter — experiment 07's nine plus Opus 4.8):
  - `deepseek/deepseek-chat-v3.1`
  - `qwen/qwen3-32b`
  - `meta-llama/llama-3.3-70b-instruct`
  - `mistralai/mistral-small-3.2-24b-instruct`
  - `openai/gpt-4o-mini`
  - `google/gemini-2.5-flash`
  - `anthropic/claude-haiku-4.5`
  - `openai/gpt-5-mini`
  - `openai/gpt-5.5`
  - `anthropic/claude-opus-4.8`
- **Forms** (three runs over the byte-identical pair set — sampling
  never consults the form): `--form two-stage`, `--form story`,
  `--form literal`, all exactly as in experiment 07. All calls at
  temperature 0.
- **Thinking mode**: `--reasoning off` only, as in experiment 07.
- **Sampling** (seed 0): 200 pairs via `--stratify-eq-ops 20
  --eq-bins 1:10` — 20 pairs per per-equation-ops bin m = 1..10, with
  **both** laws of a pair drawn from bin m (`ops_e = ops_f = m`,
  `ops_total = 2m`). Balanced pairs avoid the split-heterogeneity
  artifact experiment 07 diagnosed (its bin-4 dip came from vacuous
  laws concentrating a bin's operations in the partner law); here every
  bin is homogeneous and vacuous-free. `--label-prefix S` names pairs
  `S12-S203` after line numbers in the synthetic file, so labels cannot
  be misread as ETP numbers. Bin 1 is exhaustive: 5 laws give exactly
  the 20 ordered pairs the bin needs.
- **Other**: `max_tokens` 4096 per call, `--concurrency 8`. 200 pairs
  × 10 models = 2,000 rows per arm (6,000 rows; 8,000 calls —
  two-stage makes two per row). OpenRouter-reported cost: two-stage
  $15.94, story $6.43, literal $5.83 — $28.21 total (the two frontier
  models dominate).

Run notes:

- **Unparseable explodes with complexity and it is not truncation.**
  Pooled unparseable rates climb from 0–2% at bin 1 to 50–64% at bin
  10 in every arm, but >90% of unparseable rows finish with
  `finish_reason: stop` (story 421/460, literal 429/467, two-stage
  438/473); only ~20 per arm are `length` and ~20 `error`. Models
  complete their answers — the answers are malformed (mis-nested
  `op(` structures, prose, given-up partial lines). The 4096-token
  cap is not the binding constraint.
- Opus 4.8's 20 misses (of 600 rows) all sit at m ≥ 6, and 14 of the
  20 are its own malformed emissions (unparseable) rather than wrong
  laws — at the extreme end even the strongest model's failure mode
  shifts from misreading to losing the output grammar.
- GPT-5.5's collapse is bin-10-specific in the structured arms
  (literal 15%, two-stage 20%, both graded on `stop`-finished
  responses) while story holds 40% — the reverse of its ETP-range
  profile, where story was its weak arm.
- `correct-swapped` remains a large share of small-model correct rows
  (story 178 of 769 correct, literal 188 of 942, two-stage 211 of
  903); `correct-dualized` is 15 rows total across 6,000.

## Conclusions

- **The complexity decline continues past the ETP cap — no cliff, no
  floor, no saturation.** Pooled accuracy decays monotonically from
  ~95% (bin 1) to ~12% (bin 10) in every arm. The steepest single
  step is still the 3→4-op drop diagnosed in experiment 07 (literal
  83→53 pooled); past the cap the curves keep falling smoothly to
  near-uniform ~12% at 10 ops, where the three arms converge.
- **No ETP-memorization effect.** Bins m ≤ 4 are necessarily public
  laws (the ETP list enumerates all of them); bins m ≥ 5 cannot be.
  For the nine experiment-07 models the 4→5 step (story 33.9→22.8,
  literal 47.8→33.9, two-stage 46.1→32.2) is smaller, relatively,
  than the adjacent 3→4 step within the public range — models are not
  drawing on the published list, so the ETP-range results of
  experiments 02–07 measure reading, not recall.
- **The frontier tier splits open.** On ETP-range pairs (experiment
  07) GPT-5.5 looked saturated (95–99%). Extended to 10 ops it drops
  to 75–79.5% overall and 15–40% at bin 10, while Opus 4.8 holds
  95–98% overall and 80–100% in the top bin — the extended-complexity
  benchmark separates two models the capped benchmark could not
  distinguish. Neither reasons (0 reasoning tokens): this is a
  single-pass reading-and-transcription gap, and it is exactly where
  future frontier headroom measurements should live.
- **Experiment 07's arm ordering survives, and converges at depth.**
  Literal ≥ two-stage > story for every model except Opus 4.8, whose
  two-stage (98.0) edges its literal (97.0); pooled 47.1 / 45.2 /
  38.5. The story tax is largest in the mid bins (bin 4–7: literal
  leads story by 10–13 points) and vanishes by bin 10 (all arms 12%)
  — once the term tree is deep enough, the bottleneck is tracking the
  tree, not penetrating the narrative disguise. Two-stage tracks
  literal within ~2 points from bin 4 up; its stage-1 rewrite is as
  good as the renderer's literal text once laws have real structure,
  consistent with experiment 07's no-vacuous view.
- **The dominant high-complexity failure mode is output-grammar
  collapse, not misreading.** Wrong answers plateau while unparseable
  grows to half of all rows by bin 10 with `stop` finishes — models
  neither run out of tokens nor produce a wrong law; they fail to
  hold the nested `op(...)` syntax together. A follow-up could
  separate tree-tracking from syntax-emission (e.g. asking for the
  SSA step list instead of nested prefix terms) to locate which
  capability actually degrades.
- Methodological: balanced per-equation bins delivered the clean
  monotone curves experiment 07 had to reconstruct post hoc — no
  vacuous-law artifacts, no split-composition dips. `genform.py` +
  `--stratify-eq-ops` is the right harness for all future complexity
  work; the ETP list remains the source for anything ≤ 4 ops.
