## What these stories contain

Each story describes a workplace with a repeated action that combines two
things into one. The order of the two things matters: doing the action to
"this and that" is not assumed to give the same result as doing it to "that
and this".

Each story has two parts:

1. A custom that always holds, without exception: whenever the worker picks
   starting things and runs the described procedures, the two named results
   always come out the same.
2. A closing question, asking whether a second regularity must also always
   hold in that workplace.

## Your task

Invent a rigid, unambiguous formal notation capable of representing any
story of this kind, then represent the target story below in it. You may
choose any symbols and structure you like, as long as:

- **It is fully specified.** After reading your specification alone, someone
  else could write down, or read off, the representation of such a story
  without guessing.
- **It is unambiguous.** Every well-formed statement in your notation has
  exactly one meaning.
- **It is complete but minimal.** It captures which starting things are
  freely chosen, each performance of the combining action together with the
  order of its two participants, which results the custom says always come
  out the same, and what the closing question asks — and nothing else: no
  workers, no settings, no narrative.
- **It separates asserting from asking.** A reader of the representation
  can tell what the story asserts always holds from what it merely asks.
